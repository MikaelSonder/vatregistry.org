VAT in Hungary, known as ÁFA, is the structured transactional tax function through which taxable supplies of goods and services are classified, invoiced, reported and documented within the Hungarian and EU VAT framework. It extends beyond return filing, because businesses must determine whether Hungarian VAT registration is required, whether Hungarian VAT should appear on invoices, how deduction rights apply and how transaction evidence must be maintained.

Operationally, Hungarian VAT usually begins with business activity analysis rather than with tax form preparation. A business commonly reviews whether it is making domestic sales, imports, exports, intra-EU acquisitions, intra-EU dispatches, B2B or B2C services, platform-based transactions or mixed taxable and exempt supplies, then aligns registration, VAT number allocation, invoices, accounting codes and reporting obligations with the actual commercial flow.

The Hungarian VAT framework uses a standard VAT rate of 27% — the highest standard rate in the EU — and two reduced VAT rates of 18% and 5% for specific categories of goods and services. The 27% rate applies by default where no exemption, zero-rating or reduced-rate qualifies. Supplies at 0% cover exports and certain intra-Community supplies, and some activities are VAT-exempt. From 2026, the small-business exemption threshold rises to HUF 20 million of annual income, with further phased increases planned in subsequent years.

Cross-border relevance is substantial. For many businesses, Hungary is not an isolated VAT territory but one operational layer inside a broader European compliance environment, where warehousing, platform selling, non-resident registration, distance sales, intra-EU trade, deduction support and reporting deadlines interact as part of one tax architecture.

Definition

VAT in Hungary is the structured indirect tax function through which taxable business transactions are assessed, charged, documented and reported under Hungarian and EU VAT rules. It concerns the tax treatment of supplies, purchases and goods movements rather than business profit, and it affects domestic commerce, intra-EU trade, invoicing processes and transaction evidence.

The practical importance of the VAT function lies in its recurring operational nature. It is not limited to one registration event or one annual exercise, but instead runs continuously through sales flows, procurement processes, bookkeeping codes, invoice issuance, periodic returns and cross-border transaction control.

Country Characteristics

Country characteristics explain the jurisdiction-specific features that shape how VAT operates in Hungary. This matters because Hungarian VAT compliance depends not only on legislation, but also on administrative practice, rate structure, exemption thresholds, electronic processes and EU integration.

Hungary applies a standard VAT rate of 27% and two reduced rates of 18% and 5%, with zero-rated treatment for exports and certain intra-Community supplies and exemptions for defined activities such as health, education and financial services. The 27% rate is the default and remains the highest standard VAT rate in the European Union in 2026. The annual revenue threshold for VAT-free status rises to HUF 20 million in 2026 for resident businesses, with further increases planned, and VAT-free companies do not charge VAT and cannot reclaim VAT on their purchases.

Operational CultureHungarian VAT compliance is documentation-based, rate-sensitive and closely linked to electronic filing and NAV portal usage.

FAQ

The FAQ section collects recurring threshold questions in concise handbook form.

Is VAT in Hungary only relevant for Hungarian companies?No. Foreign companies can also need Hungarian VAT registration where operations create Hungarian VAT obligations.
Is VAT the same as corporate income tax?No. VAT is an indirect tax on supplies of goods and services, while corporate tax concerns business profit.
Are all activities automatically subject to VAT?No. Some activities are exempt, and certain supplies may involve reduced or zero-rated outcomes depending on the transaction.
What is the standard VAT rate in Hungary?27%, which is the highest standard VAT rate in the European Union in 2026.
Are there reduced rates?Yes. Hungary uses reduced rates of 18% and 5% for specific categories of goods and services.
Can resident businesses be VAT-free?Yes. Resident businesses below the HUF 20 million threshold may apply for VAT-free status, but then cannot reclaim VAT on purchases.
